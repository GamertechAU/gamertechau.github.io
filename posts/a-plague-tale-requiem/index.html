A Plague Tale: Requiem

by GamertechAU
published on 23 October 2023

Hope you don't mind rats...

NameA Plague Tale: Requiem
Completed On2023/10/23
DevelopersAsobo Studio
PublishersFocus Entertainment

Intro

A Plague Tale: Requiem is the sequel to APT: Innocence and I enjoyed it immensely. I had played Innocence prior to my streaming start so didn't replay it on stream.

Note, the series is not for people that have issues with rats. Because there's a lot of them.

Plot

Based in France 1348, you play as siblings Amicia and Hugo. Swapping between them to complete different parts of the story. It was a peaceful life, until the Fire uh... Inquisition attacked. Hugo has a strange power that affects the rats, and the brutal Inquisition wants it for reasons unknown. Of course they're willing to do whatever it takes to get it.

The player attempts to flee from them while dodging rats and learning about the power. Exploring unlocks an extremely detailed story and challenging gameplay.

Gameplay

Leave your family home and evade the soldiers. Make use of stealth, distractions and more permanent options to escape to freedom.

You can hide in long grass, throw objects to distract pursuers. Even use the environment, including the rats, to do what needs to be done to survive.

As you progress, you scavenge materials and upgrade Amicia's equipment to help you fight back. You'll need it to as there's very little room for forgiveness in this game, especially at max difficulty.

Tech

The game ran smoothly with extremely pretty graphics and good performance. There were a couple of bugs I encountered however. In the skill menu, the game shows videos for each skill when you hover over them. If you switch between the videos too quickly, the game locks up, requiring a force close and restart. Letting each video load before moving on bypasses the issue.

Also, one quest towards the middle of the game didn't spawn a required enemy. Reloading the save didn't fix it, I had to back out to the main menu and load from there. After that, I was able to progress without issue.

Recommendation

If you're not afraid of a few little rats, then I highly recommend this game series. They have excellent stories, feels and gameplay and kept chat engaged the entire time.
