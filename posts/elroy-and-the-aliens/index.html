Elroy and the Aliens

published on 7 April 2025

A charming point-and-click adventure

NameElroy and the Aliens
Completed on2025-04-07
DevelopersMotiviti
PublishersMotiviti

Playthrough

After originally being introduced to Elroy and the Aliens by the developers on Bluesky, I had to play it. Point-and-click adventure games aren't usually my thing (though I have played a fair few), but the game looked fun and the demo cemented it.

In Elroy and the Aliens you play Elroy, a young engineer/inventor and Peggy, an investigative reporter initially there to interview him. After a technical demonstration goes awry, the two begin an adventure that will take them across Earth and beyond to rescue Elroy's long-lost father.

The story and progression is very well done, and the humour throughout is excellent. Some of the steps required to progress took me a little bit, but they're all logical and the game is pretty good at telling you what people need, just need to remember them.

The point-and-click mechanics are the usual system. The player searches scenes for needed items, talks to characters for information and can combine several items to achieve the goal. If you need it, there's a hotkey to highlight areas in the scene you can interact with, though there's an achievement for not using it throughout the play through.

There's a ton of secrets and challenge achievements to unlock, multiple endings and plenty of puzzles to figure out.

The game ran perfectly out of the box with no noticeable bugs and even had a native Linux client which was greatly appreciated. The graphics were excellent, hand-drawn to extremely high detail and the audio was on point.

If you're a fan of point-and-click adventure games this is definitely a game to consider. One ending with definitely not finding all the secrets and achievements took 8.6 hours on stream.
