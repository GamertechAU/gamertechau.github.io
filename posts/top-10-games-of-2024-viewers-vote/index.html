Top 10 Video Games of 2024 - Viewers Vote

by GamertechAU
published on 25 July 2025

The top 10 games played in 2024 as voted by Twitch

In 2024, there was no shortage of new games released. As well as the good ol' backlog in the Steam Unplayed Library of Shame. But we can only experience so many of them live on stream and still have time for those pesky things like sleep.

Together, chat and I completed many games live throughout 2024 and 2025 has already blown past that number.

But the question is: Which of the games completed on stream in 2024 were engaging enough to take chat's top 10?

Chat voted, find out the results below. Starting with:

10 - Far Cry 6

Far Cry 6 review

While the game play is the usual Ubisoft fare, Far Cry 6 came out swinging with a dark, engaging story, cute animal companions and enough explosions to make Michael Bay jelly.

While attempting to escape a vicious dictatorship, Dani finds themselves dragged into the life of a resistance member. Equip yourself for a battle of survival and save the citizens of Yara. And yourself.

Chorizo is bestest boi.

9 - Star Trek: Resurgence

Star Trek: Resurgence review

Dramatic Labs thwacks their nerd-cred on the table with a Star Trek game that's actually good. Experience Trek without 'movie magic' and no predatory micro-transactions. Star Trek: Resurgence combines an original, high-quality story with Telltale-style game play.

Choose-your-own-adventure to defend the Federation from an ancient enemy bent on domination. Protect your crew, make new allies and uphold the ideals of a Starfleet officer. Or don't, it's your choice.

8 - Yakuza Kiwami

Yakuza Kiwami review

The OG Yakuza remade, Yakuza Kiwami brings Kiryu into HD with Ryu Ga Gotoku's Dragon engine.

After taking the fall for the murder of a Yakuza patriarch, Kiryu returns to a changed world. And everyone still wants to fight you. Stop a pending war between Yakuza clans with plenty of rapid attitude adjustments to the face. But don't forget to experience the vast side-story Yakuza has to offer.

7 - Final Fantasy VII Remake Intergrade

Final Fantasy VII: Remake review

The reboot of the cult hit FF7 comes out with extremely pretty graphics, an engaging story and characters that matter. Is it the FF7 remake we wanted? No, but it's still good in its own right.

Experience Cloud's edginess in fully-voiced HD as the merc gets dragged into saving the entire planet from the Shinra energy mega-corp and its heavily-armed military. Discover the truth behind Shinra's plans and fight to stop them all the while encountering all the original characters brought to life.

6 - LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga

LEGO Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga review

Star Wars, but without the mature audiences rating. LEGO Star Wars brings all the movies to PC but with the eccentricity of LEGO and lots of DLC.

Explore the Star Wars universe, encounter familiar faces, unlock many of them as playable characters and defeat the Empire. Also smash everything. Lots and lots of smashing.

5 - The Planet Crafter

The Planet Crafter review

Ever been sentenced to life in prison and get offered a chance to work it off by terraforming a planet for a dodgy corporation? Neither had I until Planet Crafter came along.

Crash-land on an uninhabitable planet. Scavenge materials and build technology to survive and eventually turn the planet green. But you weren't the first to arrive on this world...

4 - Yakuza 0

Yakuza 0 review

The prequel to the OG. Yakuza 0 is where it really starts. Join Kiryu as he gets framed for the murder of a guy he beat up and Majima as he attempts to escape from Yakuza control. All while listening to epic beats.

Explore the world flooded with engaging side-stories, save the girl and stop a war by punching everyone that gets in your way. Don't forget to loosen up at the disco from time to time.

3 - Lost Judgment

Lost Judgment review

Part two of the detective spin-off series in the Yakuza universe. Lost Judgment brings a dark and visceral story as broke private detective Yagami and friends investigate an alleged groper who confessed to murder at the exact same time.

Discover the truth behind the case while fending off Yakuza, gang members, hitmen and dodgy prosecutors without attracting the attention of people that want the past to stay buried.

By becoming a school teacher.

2 - Final Profit: A Shop RPG

Final Profit review

Coming in at number two is an extremely detailed indie shop management sim. Solo dev Brent Arnold brings a unique experience saving your kingdom from greedy capitalists, by beating them at their own game.

Take control of Madama Biz and work your way up from a small business to a major international enterprise. Master supply and demand and explore the crazy amount of optional secrets and puzzles around the world.

Earn your way into the corporate headquarters and take them down however you choose. Will you be a benevolent queen or ruthlessly control everything that gets in your way? It's your choice.

Also featuring an included optional rogue-lite mode for players wanting a lighter play through.

1 - Judgment

Judgment review

Chat's number one voted game for 2024, Judgment.

Judgment is the beginning of RGG's beat-em-up detective series. Investigate minor requests for the public as Yagami, the broke private detective and his ex-Yakuza partner Kaito. Trail cheating partners, trap panty thieves, play some old-school SEGA arcade games and compete in fast-paced drone races. Maybe even find a girlfriend or three.

When you're done with the huge amount of side stories though, don't forget to be drawn into a conspiracy at the highest levels. A conspiracy powerful people are willing to kill to keep hidden.

Conclusion

Well that's it! Chat has spoken. Do you agree? Want to ask about any of the games? Hit me up on Bluesky, or Follow on Twitch to know when we go live!
