Conker's Bad Fur Day

by GamertechAU
published on 21 March 2025

Blast from the past

NameConker's Bad Fur Day
Completed on2025-03-21
DevelopersRare
PublishersRare

Playthrough

I had previously played Conker's Bad Fur Day several times back when the N64 console was new. It was an excellent game back then and the good news is, it's still awesome! After a suggestion from chat, nostalgia kicked in and I immediately added it to the list.

Conker's BFD is an epic action-adventure/platformer released all the way back in 2001 (feel old yet?) featuring Conker, the red squirrel trying to find his way back home after a blinder down at the pub. Conker inevitably gets waylaid into numerous adventures across different biomes. Each with unique feels and game modes and ends up having one of those days.

The game features many references to popular movies, games and memes of the time. It includes exceptional and often irreverent humour with plenty of well-chosen 4th wall breaks. It also features one of the most enjoyable boss fights in gaming.

For this play through I used RetroArch with 8x upscaling. Native 240p N64 resolution doesn't work that well at 1440p, and an Xbox Elite controller. Once tweaked a bit, the game ran smoothly. Though being designed for the N64's single stick controller, it was a bit tricky to handle things like aiming.

For those interested in replicating this, ensure you configure at least these settings. Load the game, then press F1 to open settings.

  • Core Options/RDP Plugin: ParaLLEl-RDP
  • Core Options/ParaLLEl-RDP/Upscaling factor: 8x
  • Core Options/Framerate: Fullspeed

Reload the game and enjoy the fast and smooth Conker.

The audio was excellent and the graphics are reminiscent of Rare's other games such as Banjo-Kazooie. It stuttered a fair bit on RetroArch's default settings, but giving it a bit more made it run great.

Conker's Bad Fur Day is definitely a brilliant game, well worth a play through and I enjoyed going back to it. On stream it took ~12.5 hours.
