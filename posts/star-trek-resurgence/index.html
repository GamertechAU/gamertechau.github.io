Star Trek: Resurgence

published on 16 September 2024

This is Star Trek

NameStar Trek: Resurgence
Completed On2024/09/16
DevelopersDramatic Labs
PublishersBruner House

Playthrough

In a world full of half-assed Star Trek games focused on milking players' wallets rather making an actual game, Star Trek: Resurgence is a breath of fresh air. It brings back old-school Star Trek and respect for the science rather than the new movie magic stuff. (Prodigy and Lower Decks excluded. They're awesome).

Developed by ex-Telltale devs, Resurgence is a choose-your-own-adventure game with an enjoyable story. Your choices actually matter. Some of those choices can be pretty hard, but all of them help guide the story and decide your ending.

You play as two characters on the Centaur-class USS Resolute, swapping between them at different points in the story. The game has a similar relationship system to Telltale games and all the characters you interact with matter. The story itself is original and well crafted. Seamlessly linked to a previous canon mission of the Enterprise-D.

There's different mini-game mechanics used throughout the game. This includes diplomacy, tricorder scanning for discovery/repair, piloting and phaser combat. Resurgence also includes talented voice actors including Jonathan Frakes reprising his role as Riker on the USS Titan.

Tech-wise, the game ran well. It was stable and well optimised which can unfortunately be hard to find these days. Choices have a timer, but I found many of them if the timer runs out the game will freeze until you make a decision. This reduces a lot of pressure and gives time to discuss them with chat.

If you're a fan of OG Star Trek, then this game is definitely one to pick up. I enjoyed every minute of it and it engaged (pun intended) chat extremely well.
