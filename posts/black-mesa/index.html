Black Mesa

by GamertechAU
published on 18 June 2025

Worthy remake of Half Life 1

Name Black Mesa
Completed on 2025-06-18
Developers Crowbar Collective
Publishers Crowbar Collective

Play through

Having played Half Life 1 & 2 longer ago than I care to remember, it was a fun trip playing this fan-made remake.

In Black Mesa, you play Gordon Freeman, a scientist at the Black Mesa Research Facility. Your sole job seems limited to going into a dangerous area in your hazard suit and pressing a button to start off an experiment on an unknown material.

Doing so however triggers an alien invasion and the player becomes the sole force with any intention of trying to stop it. The other scientists are too scared and the military just wants to "clean" the area of any problems. Including you.

As you play through the game, you collect a wide range of weaponry and equipment. Encounter NPCs that help you progress, deal with hostile forces and discover all the secrets hidden in Black Mesa.

The game also features a large number of puzzles. None of them are particularly complicated, however spotting the things you need to interact with can be difficult as many of them are small or blend into the scenery. There is also a bug since launch in Xen where cables you need to connect are invisible. Reloading a save from before the area generally solves that.

The maps are well made and expansive. The audio worked well, though there were limited voice actors. Progressing through the story and hearing the exact same scientist voices is a little jarring.

The loading times between areas are pretty long, much slower than the original. No issues were encountered however. The game definitely got framey in some of the more open sections. Nothing visibly impacting it, likely shader caching as you move close to new areas. Recommend Windows users to load the game with the DXVK option to get much better performance. Linux users run it with Vulkan by default.

Other than that, the game ran really well with no stability issues. There is also a heap of achievements that are actually achievements. Mostly based around easter eggs throughout the game.

If you enjoyed Half Life back in the day, or into a solid puzzle shooter, then I highly recommend giving it a go.
