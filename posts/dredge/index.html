DREDGE

published on 20 February 2025

These fish bite back

NameDREDGE
Completed on2025-02-20
DevelopersBlack Salt Games
PublishersTeam 17

Playthrough

An extremely well-made game. Played with the Pale Reach and Iron Rig DLC.

In DREDGE, you play a fisherman who accepted a job as an angler for a remote island chain. A nice peaceful job...Except this is a Lovecraft game.

Putter around the ocean fishing up a huge (230) range of fish in different biomes to sell for income or to fill special orders, but be back before sundown as these fish bite back. There's a ton of hidden quests hidden around the map, plenty of NPC's to talk to with stories, most of whom need your help to find various fish and items.

As you explore you find or unlock upgrades to your boat, including faster, more redundant motors (you'll need em), specialised fishing equipment that can operate in more areas, more powerful lights, more cargo space, etc. You don't need all of them to complete the game, but you will need a lot.

You'll also find new hungry things interested in you (BIG goober fish), and natural events that don't mix well with your boat such as water spouts. Each biome has its own hazards and all of them increase your stress, which as it gets higher seems to attract more things to you.

As the story progresses, you'll learn about and potentially encounter a number of cosmic horrors with plenty of Lovecraft-inspired lore to find.

DREDGE ran perfectly with no noticeable issues and has a Platinum rating on ProtonDB. The audio was excellent and heightened the immersion of the game and the art style fit the mood perfectly. The devs put a lot of effort into it.

Highly recommend giving this a go. The DLC if you get them are best played from the early-mid game rather than saving until the end to keep it balanced. Obtained the base game from an Epic freebie.
