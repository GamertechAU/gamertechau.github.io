The Planet Crafter

by GamertechAU
published on 24 May 2024

Prison vs terraforming a planet

NameThe Planet Crafter
Completed On2024/05/24
DevelopersMiju Games
PublishersMiju Games

Intro

An interesting planet terraformer. It also includes optional co-op that was added after my first play through.

Plot

You play as a criminal who chose to commute their sentence in return for terraforming an entire planet for the ruling corpos. You succeed and you're free to go. Easy, right? Just terraform a whole planet, what could go wrong?

Turns out, quite a bit. It seems you weren't the first to arrive. There's a bit of a lore-based story in the game as you learn about the planet and its history. Can't say much more without spoilers, but just enjoy exploring the world.

Gameplay

The gameplay itself is solid. Choose a preset planet, with more available with DLC. After landing, the player must collect materials and build tech to increase the terraforming level of the planet. The end goal being to make it habitable for humanity and profit.

The game is peaceful so there's no hostiles to deal with, just the environment and lack of atmosphere. As you progress your metrics, the corpo sends you blueprints for new upgraded tech.

The science is...interesting as the math is slightly impractical. The planetary heat starts at 0 Kelvin, aka absolute zero where no matter can move. Even when terraforming is 'complete' the heat is at 'an electron just twitched' level. The air pressure can get pretty lethal as well. Also when you're done you can just keep going, just keep increasing the metrics to absurd levels.

Tech

The UI is easy to understand and use. The game is stable and runs smoothly, though could do with some more optimisation. The graphics are fairly simple but pretty.

Parts of the map are lacking collision mesh so exploring a bit too wildly can clip you inside of mountains. Luckily it's easy to get back out.

The game has a Gold rating on ProtonDB. While it runs out of the box, some users on obsolete software have given it negative reports from time to time, impacting the score. The Planet Crafter is also Steam Deck Verified.

Recommendation

This is a fun and reasonably chill game with a small but well-developed storyline. If you want to just chillax by yourself or with friends, then it's definitely a good buy.
